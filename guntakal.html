Guntakal Division

The South Coast Railway Women’s Welfare Organisation (SCoRWWO) is a registered welfare and social service organization working under South Coast Railway for the welfare of railway employees, women staff, children, and railway families in the Guntakal Division.

About Guntakal Division

The Guntakal Division is one of the oldest and largest railway divisions in South India. Headquartered at Guntakal, the division occupies a strategic position in the railway network by connecting multiple states including Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Maharashtra. It serves as a major railway junction and plays a critical role in long-distance passenger and freight transportation. The division is well known for its extensive railway network and its ability to handle large volumes of freight traffic. It supports the transportation of minerals, coal, cement, iron ore, food grains, fertilizers, petroleum products, and other essential commodities. The division serves several industrial and mining regions, making it an important contributor to economic activities and railway revenue generation. Guntakal Division operates through several important railway stations such as Guntakal Junction, Anantapur, Kurnool City, Dharmavaram Junction, Adoni, and Raichur. These stations serve millions of passengers annually and provide connectivity to major metropolitan cities across India. The division continuously focuses on infrastructure modernization, electrification, safety enhancement, and passenger service improvements. Its strong railway network and operational efficiency make it one of the most significant divisions supporting railway transportation in southern India.
